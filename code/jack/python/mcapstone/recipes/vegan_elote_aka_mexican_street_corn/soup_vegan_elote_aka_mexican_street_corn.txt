<p>My favorite food growing up was corn. My favorite food as a grown up is <em>this</em> <em>corn</em>. It is sweet and crisp with just the right amount of chili and lime. Vegan parmesan takes it over the top. And if you're me, extra cilantro and lime juice are a must. </p>
<p>It's sweet, salty, creamy, savory, summer side dish perfection!</p>
<h2>Reasons to love this recipe!</h2>
<ul><li>Can be made outdoors on a grill or inside with a cast iron grill pan.</li><li>Is vegan, gluten free, and secretly healthy!</li><li>Comes together in under 30 minutes and pairs with so many dishes.</li></ul>

<h2>Ingredients and Substitutions:</h2>
<ul><li>Corn - <a href="https://www.dudafresh.com/products/tag/dandy-sweet-corn" rel="nofollow sponsored" target="_blank">Dandy Sweet Corn</a> is incredibly sweet, crisp, and perfect for this recipe.</li><li><a href="https://www.darngoodveggies.com/vegan-parmesan/" rel="">Vegan parmesan</a> - recipe included below, or grab some vegan store-bought parmesan. For the homemade version, you'll need <a href="https://amzn.to/3ekCwmI" rel="nofollow sponsored" target="_blank">raw cashews</a>, <a href="https://amzn.to/3h56fC0" rel="nofollow sponsored" target="_blank">nutritional yeast</a>, garlic powder, and salt.</li><li><a href="https://amzn.to/3umFsEQ" rel="nofollow sponsored" target="_blank">Vegan mayo</a> - or regular mayo if not avoiding eggs.</li><li><a href="https://amzn.to/3h5UsD9" rel="nofollow sponsored" target="_blank">Ancho chili powder</a> - makes the BEST elote. It is smoky, sweet, and only mildly spicy. You can also use <a href="https://amzn.to/3uokk16" rel="nofollow sponsored" target="_blank">guajillo chili powder</a>. In a pinch, use regular chili powder.</li><li>Garlic - one clove or substitute ⅛th teaspoon garlic powder.</li><li>Lime juice - fresh squeezed only! Plus wedges for serving.</li><li><a href="https://amzn.to/3nQJaUQ" rel="nofollow sponsored" target="_blank">Chipotle powder</a> - optional but adds another layer of mild smoky spice.</li><li>Salt - to bring out the flavors.</li><li>Cilantro - for flavor and color!</li></ul>
<h2>How to make vegan elote:</h2>
<p><strong>Make the vegan parmesan:</strong> Pulse the cashews, nutritional yeast, garlic powder and salt in a small food processor (or blender) until it resembles grated parm.</p>
<p><strong>Stir together the chili mayo:</strong> Add the ingredients to a bowl and stir until smooth.</p>
<p><strong>Grill the corn:</strong> Place the corn on a medium-high grill, or grill pan, and cook on all sides until the corn is cooked through and has prominent grill marks.</p>
<p><strong>Assemble and enjoy</strong>: Spread the corn with chili mayo, sprinkle with parmesan, ancho chili powder, and cilantro. Enjoy!</p>

<h2>Storage?</h2>
<p><strong>Fridge:</strong> Store any leftover corn and chili mayo separately in the fridge. Store parmesan at room temperature for 1-2 months and in the fridge for up to 4 months. </p>
<p>Reheat the corn before adding toppings.</p>
<p><strong>Freezer:</strong> freezer storage is not recommended for this recipe.</p>
<h2>Recipe variations:</h2>
<ul><li>No grill? No problem! A <a href="https://amzn.to/33mhGgt" rel="nofollow sponsored" target="_blank">cast iron grill pan</a> works great! They are smaller in size, so note that you will likely have to cook the corn in batches.</li><li>Can't find ancho chili powder? Use chili powder instead, but start small and work your way up. Most store-bought chili powders are spicier than ancho chili powder.</li></ul>

<div class="tasty-recipes-instructions">
<div class="tasty-recipes-instructions-header">
<h3 data-tasty-recipes-customization="h3-color.color h3-transform.text-transform">Instructions</h3>
</div>
<div class="tasty-recipes-instructions-body" data-tasty-recipes-customization="body-color.color">
<p id="instruction-step-1"><strong>Vegan parmesan:</strong> Pulse the cashews, nutritional yeast, garlic powder and salt in a small food processor (or blender) until it resembles grated parm.</p>
<p id="instruction-step-2"><strong>Chili mayo:</strong> In a bowl, stir together the mayo, ancho chili powder, garlic, lime juice, optional chipotle powder and salt until smooth.</p>
<p id="instruction-step-3"><strong>Corn:</strong> Place the corn on a medium-high grill, or grill pan, and cook on all sides until the corn is cooked and has grill marks, about 4 minutes per side.</p>
<p><!-- wp:paragraph /--></p>
<p><!-- wp:paragraph /--></p>
<p><!-- wp:paragraph /--></p>
<p><!-- wp:paragraph /--></p>
<p id="instruction-step-4"><strong>Assemble</strong>: Spread the corn with chili mayo, sprinkle with parmesan, ancho chili powder, and cilantro. Enjoy!</p>
</div>
