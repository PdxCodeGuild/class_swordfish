moat-id="recipes/full-recipe"> <div class="full-recipe" data-id="12298" id="full-recipe"> <span itemprop="name" style="display:none;">Carrot Apple Soup </span> <span itemprop="recipeYield" style="display:none;">6.000</span> <div class="recipe-image left"> <img alt="" itemprop="image" src="https://www.washingtonpost.com/resizer/9hmQgvSuGc4c_rJqFOy9JRwHe6E=/arc-anglerfish-washpost-prod-washpost/public/OFP2WXRY7AI63OFPBICOLXB5WY.jpg" title="Carrot Apple Soup "/> <p class="pb-caption">Scott Suchman for The Washington Post; food styling by Lisa Cherkasky for The Washington Post</p> </div> <h5> <meta content="2011-09-07" itemprop="datePublished"/><span class="timestamp">Sep 7, 2011</span> </h5> <div class="article-content"> <div class="recipe-summary" itemprop="description"> <p>The apple in this simple, scrumptious soup will make the sweet carrots and onions even sweeter. Those are all fall ingredients, which makes this the perfect back-to-school lunch choice.</p> <p>You'll need a cutting board, a sharp knife (and an adult to wield it), measuring spoons, a pot with a lid, a slotted spoon, a wooden mixing spoon, a measuring cup and a blender or food processor.</p> <p><span class="heading">Storage Notes: </span> Refrigerate for up to 3 days.</p> </div> <hr/> <div class="ingredients cf"> <h5>Servings: 6 </h5> <p><span class="heading2">Yield:</span> <span itemprop="recipeYield">Makes 12 1/4 cups</span></p> <h5>Ingredients</h5> <ul class="ingredient-list" data-id="nogrouping"> <li><div class="measurement" itemprop="ingredients">1 tablespoon olive or canola oil</div></li> <li><div class="measurement" itemprop="ingredients">1 medium yellow onion, chopped</div></li> <li><div class="measurement" itemprop="ingredients">2 pounds carrots, peeled and cut crosswise into thin slices</div></li> <li><div class="measurement" itemprop="ingredients">1 apple, peeled (optional), cored and chopped, plus more for garnish</div></li> <li><div class="measurement" itemprop="ingredients">1/2 teaspoon fine salt, plus more to taste</div></li> <li><div class="measurement" itemprop="ingredients">8 cups no-salt-added chicken or vegetable broth</div></li> <li><div class="measurement" itemprop="ingredients">1/2 cup Greek-style or plain low-fat yogurt (optional)</div></li> </ul> </div> <hr/> <h5>Directions</h5> <div itemprop="recipeInstructions"> <p>Warm a large pot over medium heat until hot. Carefully add the oil, followed by the onion, carrots and apple. Stir to coat the ingredients in the oil, season with salt, then cover and cook until the ingredients start to soften, about 15 minutes. From time to time, lift the lid and stir with a wooden spoon.</p> <p>Increase the heat to high, add the chicken or vegetable broth and bring to a boil. Reduce the heat to low, and cook until the carrots are completely tender, about 20 minutes. Remove from the heat and let cool for 20 minutes.</p> <p>Using a slotted spoon, transfer the solids to the blender or food processor and process until smooth. Add the yogurt, if desired, and process again, then stir this mixture back into the soup. Taste, and season with additional salt, if desired.</p> <p>Serve right away, or cover and refrigerate until needed.</p> </div> <hr/> <h5>Recipe Source</h5> <p>From Sally Sampson; adapted from the Fall 2011 issue of ChopChop magazine. </p> <p><em>Tested by Bonnie S.
